# Sales Page Contract

## Data separation

Never use an internal story brief as public product copy. Keep separate fields for:

- internal generation brief;
- customer-facing short hook;
- customer-facing long description;
- verified metadata;
- curated preview pages.

## Public sanitation

Remove HTML comments, Markdown control syntax, page keys, prompt notes, callbacks, story rules, debugging output, model metadata, and internal buyer-analysis labels.

## Verified metadata

After final PDF approval, derive and persist:

- final page count;
- unique illustration count;
- actual story word count and read-aloud time;
- age range and category;
- final cover/mockup URLs;
- price and included features.

Public values must match the final version, not a draft.

## Preview strategy

Use 3–5 curated spreads that demonstrate:

1. protagonist appeal;
2. inciting problem;
3. escalating fun or emotion;
4. participation/re-read value;
5. quality without revealing the full ending.

Do not expose repetitive pages, broken assets, raw page comments, or a wall of draft text.

## Conversion structure

- Parent pain/question hook
- One-sentence story promise
- Child benefits and parent benefits
- Age/read-aloud/page badges from verified data
- Curated preview
- Clear CTA
- Accurate delivery/refund/security statements

Hide claims such as audience size, guarantee, encryption, or delivery timing unless they are implemented and evidenced.
